User Needs and Requirements

User Needs:

  • Portability and Lightweight: The device must be easy to carry and not add significant weight to campers' gear.
  • Durability and Weather Resistance: It should withstand outdoor conditions such as rain, dust, and varying temperatures.
  • Ease of Use: Simplicity in setup and operation is crucial, considering the diverse age and tech-savviness of campers.
  • Low Maintenance: The alarm should require minimal maintenance, with long-lasting batteries or alternative power sources like solar.
  • Loud and Clear Alarm: The alarm sound must be loud enough to be heard over natural outdoor noises, with options for different sounds indicating various levels of alerts.
  • Sensitivity and Accuracy: It should accurately detect fire and smoke with minimal false alarms to prevent unnecessary panic.
  • Connectivity Options: Although it's designed for use in remote areas, options for connectivity to smartphones or local authorities could be beneficial for emergency situations.
  • Affordability: The product should be cost-effective, providing value for the safety it offers without being prohibitively expensive.
  • Environmental Impact: Considering the camping setting, the product should be environmentally friendly, avoiding harm to wildlife and nature.
  • Educational Material: Including instructions on fire safety and prevention specific to camping environments.

Requirements:

Physical Requirements: Compact, rugged design with water and dust resistance (IP rating).

Technical Requirements: Advanced smoke and heat sensors, long battery life, solar charging capabilities, loudspeaker for alarms.

Operational Requirements: User-friendly interface, possibly with LED indicators for status checks, easy to activate and deactivate.

Safety and Regulatory Requirements: Compliance with safety standards and certifications relevant to camping gear and fire safety equipment.
